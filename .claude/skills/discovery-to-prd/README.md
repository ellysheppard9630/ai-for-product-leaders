# Discovery to PRD

## Purpose
Transforms messy, fragmented discovery inputs into a structured, decision-useful PRD direction. Synthesizes scattered notes, stakeholder requests, research findings, and early requirements into a clear product problem statement, requirement draft, and recommended next steps.

## Use Cases
- You have rough notes from a discovery session and need to turn them into something a PRD can be written from
- Stakeholder asks are coming in from multiple directions and you need to synthesize them into a coherent product direction
- You have early requirements that are contradictory, incomplete, or too vague to hand to engineering

## Inputs
Any of the following — alone or in combination:
- Rough notes or meeting summaries
- Stakeholder requests or asks
- Current-state workflow descriptions
- Problem statements or pain-point lists
- Research findings or support themes
- Pasted document fragments

## Outputs
A structured document covering:
1. Discovery summary (problem, user, context, pain points)
2. Findings grouped by theme
3. Confirmed facts vs assumptions vs open questions
4. Data and workflow implications
5. QA and testability considerations
6. Product direction framing
7. PRD-ready requirement draft
8. Scope and non-scope boundaries
9. Open questions and risks
10. Recommended next step
